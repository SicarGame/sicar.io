Community Update

A Statement on the SICAR Token

June 16, 2026

I owe everyone a clear and honest statement about what happened with the SICAR token.

First, I am sorry. I failed to communicate properly with my partner, Drake/Arma, and with the community before allowing this to move forward. That was my responsibility, and I understand why people feel hurt, confused, and betrayed.

I want to be clear: my intention was never to turn Sicar.io into a crypto cash grab or to use the game to scam anyone. Sicar.io has been my life for 6 years. I agreed to this because it was proposed to me by people I had known for years, people I considered friends, and people I trusted. I believed them when they said this could help bring attention to the game and potentially support development while we continue bootstrapping.

The marketing push around the token was meant to be short-term. Crypto was never supposed to become the face of Sicar.io or replace the game itself. The goal, as I understood it, was to explore a long-term economy that could support the future of the project.

That does not change the fact that this was handled badly.

After the launch, serious things happened that I did not expect: the planning group chat was deleted, there was a lack of transparency around wallets, sales, and profits, and the token collapsed. I did not make any money from this launch.

I understand why people are angry. I also understand that trust has been damaged. I am not going to publicly accuse, dox, or name anyone while this may become a legal matter, but I am preserving all relevant records, including Discord transcripts, messages, wallet information, and transaction history so this can be reviewed properly.

Effective immediately

  1. Sicar.io is pausing all crypto/token promotion.
  2. No future crypto-related decision will happen without clear written agreement with my partner and full transparency to the community.
  3. I am focusing back on the game itself.
  4. I am seeking proper advice before saying more about the people involved.

To Drake/Arma: I am sorry. I should have spoken to you sooner and made sure you were fully included before anything happened. You deserved better from me as my partner.

To the community: I am sorry I let this happen under the Sicar name. I never intended to hurt anyone. I trusted people I thought were my friends, handled the situation poorly, and I understand that I have to earn trust back through actions, not words.

Sicar.io still matters to me. The game still matters to me. I am going to do everything I can to protect it, repair what I can, and be more transparent moving forward.

Follow-up clarification

I also want to make something very clear: Armaccord/Drake had nothing to do with the SICAR token launch.

He was not involved in the planning, promotion, launch, wallet decisions, or anything related to the token. He was not aware of what was happening before it moved forward, and he did not approve it.

Drake has made it clear to me that he does not want his name connected to any crypto-related situation, and I respect that. Any anger or criticism about the token should not be directed at him.

This was my failure to communicate with him properly as my partner, and I take responsibility for that. Please do not involve him in this or treat him as responsible for something he had no part in.

On why I went quiet

I also want to explain why I was not responding to messages.

This whole thing moved extremely fast. The crypto/token idea started around 6/10, and it is now 6/16. During that time, I was either at work or spending almost every hour on my computer trying to build and implement the actual economic system I wanted Sicar to release.

I was not sitting back watching Discord and ignoring people. I was basically offline from the community side because I trusted the people handling the token/publicity to manage that part while I focused on development.

That was a mistake.

I should not have stepped away from communication during something this serious. I should have been present, checking what was being said, answering concerns, and making sure the community understood what was happening.

Instead, everything was rushed, communication broke down, and it became a mess. I take responsibility for that.

I am sorry to everyone who messaged me and felt ignored. I was overwhelmed, working, and coding nonstop, but that does not excuse the lack of communication.
